# Gates of Aker

This document was created with the assistance of an AI.

## What the Gates are
The Gates are both:
- **Places** (geographic loci with anomalies)
- **States** (a “thinness” value in the world simulation)
- **Story engine** (the source of escalating stakes)

## The white-light nodes
As discussed: a single enormous white light appears as a nodal opposite on each side of the Gates.
- Mechanically: two anchor points for anomaly propagation and containment.
- Visually: iconic, legible landmarking.

## Chaos leakage model (mechanical)
Each gate region has:
- **Thinness** (baseline)
- **Entropy Pressure** (from miracles, conflict, scarcity)
- **Containment** (structures, rituals, wards)
- **Attention** (Chaos “interest” in this world)
The system outputs:
- anomaly spawn probability
- corruption spread rate
- “black butterfly” events

## Black butterflies
### Representation
- A shard/agent that can attach to:
  - a person (heart),
  - a place (shrine),
  - a concept (law, taboo),
  - or an object (relic).

### Gameplay effects (examples)
- Grants insight/creativity but increases instability
- Introduces new needs or compulsions
- Enables “impossible” craftsmanship at a cost
- Allows Chaos to speak through dreams/omens

### Removal/containment
- Ritual cleansing (champion-led)
- Interdicts (pantheon)
- Sacrificial sealing (hard choice)
- Acceptance/integration (becoming a new kind of society)

## Gate progression arc
- Early: localized anomalies, learn containment
- Mid: factional disputes, rival gods intervene
- Late: multi-front crises; miracles become necessary and dangerous
